**AHMEDABAD:** Gujarat’s agricultural landscape is witnessing a massive early shift toward "white gold" as the current **Kharif cotton sowing season** kicks off with unprecedented momentum. According to the latest sowing data released by the Gujarat State Agriculture Department, the state has recorded a near threefold increase in early-stage cultivation compared to the same period in the previous year.

As of mid-June, farmers across Gujarat have already planted cotton over **93,499 hectares**, compared to just 34,011 hectares during the corresponding initial window last year. Industry experts credit this massive head start to favorable early pre-monsoon showers and highly lucrative central policy updates.

### Increased MSP and Lower Risk Drive Farmer Preference

A primary catalyst behind this year’s rapid acreage expansion is the central government's aggressive revision of the **Minimum Support Price (MSP)**. For the current marketing season, policy updates have established solid support floors:

- **Medium Staple Cotton:** Fixed at **₹8,267 per quintal** (an increase of ₹557).
- **Long Staple Cotton:** Fixed at **₹8,667 per quintal**.

This strong financial safety net, combined with the fact that cotton requires comparatively less water during unexpected mid-season dry spells compared to heavy alternative crops like paddy or maize, has made it the crop of choice for growers looking to manage climate risks.

### Central India Shifting Dynamics

The Cotton Association of India (CAI) projects that if current trends hold through the primary June–July monsoon window, overall cotton acreage across Central India could jump by **15%**, potentially pushing nationwide cultivation past the 130 lakh hectare mark.

While Northern hubs like Punjab and Haryana are showing slight early deficits due to pest management shifts, Gujarat and parts of Rajasthan are handily offsetting those numbers. Ground level agencies, including the Cotton Corporation of India (CCI), are adjusting their strategies accordingly—including reducing spot sale prices on older stocks by roughly ₹2,500 per candy to clear market pipelines and accommodate the incoming high-volume crop.

Agronomists are now advising regional farmers to focus on uniform seed-spacing and proactive pink bollworm management during these early vegetative phases to lock in high-quality yields.
