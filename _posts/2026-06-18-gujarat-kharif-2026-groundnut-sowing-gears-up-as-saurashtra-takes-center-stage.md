**AHMEDABAD:** As the Southwest monsoon marks its arrival across Western India, Gujarat—the country’s powerhouse for oilseed production—has officially launched its execution framework for the **Karif 2026 groundnut sowing season**.

Following a high-level review by the Gujarat State Agriculture Department, target acreages have been revised to maximize yield efficiency, with the semi-arid, highly suitable belts of the **Saurashtra region** designated as the primary focal point for peanut production this year.

### High-Yield Quality Focus & Seed Infrastructure

Learning from previous seasons where localized climate fluctuations heavily impacted overall crop yield, the state's strategy for 2026 focuses strictly on input quality and modern irrigation.

- **Certified Seed Distribution:** The Gujarat State Seeds Corporation has established dedicated distribution hubs across major agricultural districts to ensure steady availability of highly resilient, certified seed varieties (including popular regional cultivars like GAUG and Kadiri strains).
- **Micro-Irrigation Expansion:** Under the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY), the government is fast-tracking micro-irrigation infrastructure. While Saurashtra leads the volume target, North Gujarat is receiving specialized localized micro-irrigation setups to guard against uneven early monsoon dry spells.

### District-Level Outlook

Industry bodies like the Solvent Extractors' Association (SEA) and regional trade networks are monitoring ground-level preparations closely. Groundnut production hubs like **Rajkot** and **Jamnagar**—which showcased strong productivity gains in recent seasonal cycles reaching averages up to 2,250–2,800 kg per hectare—remain the critical engines for the 2026 market projections.

Conversely, coastal districts like Amreli, Gir Somnath, and Junagadh are being closely supported with crop advisories from local Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs) to mitigate potential weather anomalies.

With steady procurement frameworks ready via agencies like NAFED and local APMCs, the trade sentiment surrounding Gujarat’s groundnut complex remains highly optimistic at the opening of this new seasonal chapter.
